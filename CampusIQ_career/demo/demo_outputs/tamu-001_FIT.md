## 2. ROLE RECOMMENDATIONS

*Career direction is currently open, so five paths are presented below — each grounded in your existing profile. These are options to evaluate, not a ranked list.*

---

### PATH 1 · Business Systems Analyst
**Fit signal: Strong**

> *Bridges business process and technology — the core of what MIS trains you for*

**What this role does:** Works between business stakeholders and technical teams to document processes, gather requirements, test systems, and improve workflows. Common in finance, healthcare, logistics, and corporate tech environments.

**Why it fits you:**
- MIS degree is a direct pipeline into this role
- SQL and Salesforce basics are directly applicable
- Problem solving and written communication are the two soft skills most commonly listed in BSA job postings

**What you'd still need to build:**
- Process documentation experience (often picked up in internships)
- Familiarity with ERP systems (SAP, Oracle) — both appear frequently in DFW postings
- Any exposure to project management frameworks (Agile, Scrum) would strengthen candidacy

**DFW employers actively hiring this profile:** AT&T, Deloitte, CBRE, Lennox International, Amazon

**Typical entry-level title:** Business Systems Analyst, Junior Business Analyst, IT Business Analyst

---

### PATH 2 · Data Analyst (Business-Facing)
**Fit signal: Strong, with a skills gap to close**

> *Translates data into business decisions — your analytics minor points here*

**What this role does:** Pulls, cleans, and interprets data to surface trends and recommendations. Works in finance, operations, marketing, or dedicated analytics teams. Output is usually dashboards, reports, and presentations to non-technical stakeholders.

**Why it fits you:**
- Business Analytics minor is a direct signal to recruiters for this track
- SQL and Excel are the starting point for most entry-level data analyst work
- Your interests in data, finance, and operations map cleanly to the three most common verticals for this role in DFW

**What you'd still need to build:**
- Tableau or Power BI — these appear in the majority of DFW data analyst postings and you have neither yet. This is the most important technical gap to close before graduation.
- Python (pandas, basic data manipulation) — increasingly expected even at entry level
- A portfolio project or two that shows analysis end-to-end, not just coursework

**DFW employers actively hiring this profile:** Fisher Investments, Dell, HCA Healthcare, Amazon, Deloitte

**Typical entry-level title:** Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, Reporting Analyst

---

### PATH 3 · CRM / Salesforce Administrator or Analyst
**Fit signal: Moderate-to-Strong — a more specific but high-demand track**

> *Salesforce skills are scarce at the entry level; your early exposure is a differentiator*

**What this role does:** Manages and optimizes Salesforce environments for sales, marketing, or service teams. Involves data entry standards, user support, reporting, and system configuration. Often sits inside operations, revenue operations, or IT departments.

**Why it fits you:**
- You already have Salesforce basics — most entry-level candidates don't
- Salesforce appears directly in DFW's most-required skills list
- Your interest in operations and finance aligns with where Salesforce roles live organizationally

**What you'd still need to build:**
- Salesforce Administrator certification (ADM-201) — this is achievable before graduation and would materially improve your competitiveness. Salesforce offers free training through Trailhead.
- Understanding of sales or marketing operations process context
- This path is narrower by design; it trades breadth for a specific, in-demand credential

**DFW employers actively hiring this profile:** Fisher Investments, AT&T, CBRE, and most large enterprise firms with sales operations functions

**Typical entry-level title:** Salesforce Administrator, CRM Analyst, Revenue Operations Analyst

---

### PATH 4 · Financial Analyst (Operations or Corporate Finance Track)
**Fit signal: Moderate**

> *Your finance interest and accounting coursework open this door, but competition is high*

**What this role does:** Supports budgeting, forecasting, variance analysis, and financial reporting. Corporate finance track tends to sit in FP&A (Financial Planning & Analysis). Operations finance track focuses on cost analysis and efficiency metrics for business units.

**Why it fits you:**
- Financial Accounting coursework is a prerequisite for this track
- Business Statistics and Excel are directly applicable
- Finance is one of your stated interest areas
- DFW is home to multiple financial services firms actively hiring entry-level analysts

**What you'd still need to build:**
- This path is more competitive at the entry level than paths 1–3; finance analysts often come from Finance or Accounting majors
- Excel modeling skills need to go beyond the basics — financial modeling, pivot tables, and scenario analysis are expected
- A finance-focused internship would significantly strengthen your candidacy here
- MIS gives you a differentiation angle (financial systems, data fluency) — lean into that in positioning

**DFW employers actively hiring this profile:** Fisher Investments, Deloitte, HCA Healthcare, Lennox International

**Typical entry-level title:** Financial Analyst, FP&A Analyst, Operations Finance Analyst

---

### PATH 5 · Supply Chain / Operations Analyst
**Fit signal: Moderate**

> *Operations is a stated interest, and DFW's logistics infrastructure makes this viable*

**What this role does:** Analyzes supply chain data, tracks inventory or vendor performance, supports process improvement projects, and works with ERP systems. Common in manufacturing, retail, logistics, and healthcare.

**Why it fits you:**
- Operations is one of your three stated interest areas
- SQL and Excel are the foundational tools for entry-level supply chain analytics work
- DFW's position as a major logistics and distribution hub means real volume of openings in this category

**What you'd still need to build:**
- ERP system exposure (SAP, Oracle) — appears consistently in supply chain postings
- Some understanding of supply chain fundamentals — consider whether your remaining electives could include a relevant course
- This path would benefit from an internship at a firm with physical operations (retail, manufacturing, healthcare)

**DFW employers actively hiring this profile:** Amazon, Dell, Lennox International, HCA Healthcare

**Typical entry-level title:** Supply Chain Analyst, Operations Analyst, Inventory Analyst
